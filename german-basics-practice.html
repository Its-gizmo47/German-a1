A consolidated practice set for everything you've covered so far: the alphabet, greetings and farewells, saying your name, origin, and residence, and asking/telling how someone is doing.

The Alphabet

  • German has 26 standard letters plus four extra: ä, ö, ü, ß.
  • ß (Eszett/scharfes S) sounds like a sharp "s" — used after long vowels/diphthongs (e.g. Straße).
  • Letter names differ from English — e is "eh", i is "ee", j is "yot", v is "fow", w is "veh".

Greetings & Farewells

  • Hallo and Guten Tag both work any time of day — Hallo is casual, Guten Tag is neutral/polite.
  • Time-specific greetings: Guten Morgen (morning), Guten Abend (evening).
  • Tschüss is the casual goodbye; Auf Wiedersehen is the formal one.
Guten Morgen!Good morning!
Guten Tag!Good day / Hello!
Guten Abend!Good evening!
Tschüss! / Auf Wiedersehen!Bye! / Goodbye! (formal)
3

Saying Your Name

  • Wie heißt du? (informal) / Wie heißen Sie? (formal) — "What's your name?"
  • Answer with Ich heiße... or the shorter Ich bin....
Wie heißt du?What's your name? (informal)
Ich heiße Ali.My name is Ali.
Wie heißen Sie?What's your name? (formal)
Ich bin Frau Schmidt.I'm Mrs. Schmidt.
4

Saying Where You're From

  • Woher kommst du? — "Where do you come from?"
  • Answer: Ich komme aus... + country/city (aus takes dative, but country names don't usually change at A1).
Woher kommst du?Where are you from?
Ich komme aus Pakistan.I come from Pakistan.
Woher kommt sie?Where does she come from?
Sie kommt aus Deutschland.She comes from Germany.
5

Saying Where You Live

  • Wo wohnst du? — "Where do you live?"
  • Answer: Ich wohne in... + city.
  • Note the difference: kommen aus (origin) vs. wohnen in (current residence) — you can come from one place and live in another.
Wo wohnst du?Where do you live?
Ich wohne in Sargodha.I live in Sargodha.
Wohnst du in Lahore?Do you live in Lahore?
Nein, ich wohne in Sargodha.No, I live in Sargodha.
6

Asking & Telling Wellbeing

  • Wie geht es dir? (informal) / Wie geht es Ihnen? (formal) — "How are you?"
  • This structure uses dative dir/Ihnen — literally "how goes it to you".
  • Common answers: Mir geht es gut/schlecht., or simply Gut, danke. / Und dir? to ask back.
Wie geht es dir?How are you? (informal)
Mir geht es gut, danke.I'm doing well, thanks.
Und dir?And you?
Mir geht es nicht so gut.I'm not doing so well.
